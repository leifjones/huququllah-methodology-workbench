# Considered Approaches and Current Posture

**Status:** Research decision log; every posture is revisable in light of further study, consultation, or better examples

**Purpose:** Preserve approaches that have shaped this project, the problems they attempted to solve, and the reasons they are currently used, tested, or set aside

This document does not declare a final personal methodology or an authoritative interpretation. Recorded financial facts should remain reproducible while the meaning assigned to them may be reconsidered. A later posture supersedes an earlier interpretation without pretending that the earlier calculation, payment, or rate record never existed.

## 1. Single primary currency with event-based augmentation

### What it proposes

For a person whose financial life is principally conducted in one currency, a methodology profile identifies one **primary currency**. Native-currency facts are never relabeled, but assessable amounts spanning currencies may be translated into the primary currency at a documented assessment date.

Gold determines the local-currency value of each complete nineteen-mithqál threshold unit at the relevant assessment. Previously assessed principal is carried through the person's history without treating a later gold-price change as an acquisition event. Further liability is associated with qualifying augmentation such as additional income after expenses, subsequently acquired possessions, realized profit, recovery beyond a prior loss, or a relevant change of ownership.

### Source-grounded rationale

- [Q&A 8](https://www.bahai.org/library/authoritative-texts/bahaullah/kitab-i-aqdas/9) describes later liability in terms of what accrues to previously assessed principal and identifies the increase as realized profit.
- [Note 125](https://www.bahai.org/library/authoritative-texts/bahaullah/kitab-i-aqdas/15) describes income after expenses increasing the value of possessions by another prescribed amount.
- [Paragraph 50 of the application compilation](https://www.bahai.org/library/authoritative-texts/compilations/huququllah-right-god/3) refers to profit that is an addition to capital and says that a particular sum is not repeatedly subject.
- The [codification](https://www.bahai.org/library/authoritative-texts/compilations/codification-law-huququllah/4) connects further units with subsequently acquired possessions, distinguishes recovery from augmentation, and defers payment on appreciation until the increase is realized.
- [Paragraph 72 of the application compilation](https://www.bahai.org/library/authoritative-texts/compilations/huququllah-right-god/3) calls for computation based on accumulated savings so that losses and realized investment results can be recognized across periods.

Taken together, these passages support a **derived design rule** for the simple case: when nominal assessable property in the primary currency is unchanged and no acquisition, income, realized profit, realized appreciation, recovery beyond prior loss, or ownership event has occurred, a gold-price movement alone does not create a new augmentation event in the ordinary lens.

### Limits

No located source directly answers the exact case of unchanged cash gaining value relative to gold. The conclusion above is a source-grounded synthesis, not an explicit ruling about currency or gold-price movements. It does not yet resolve:

- Holdings in several currencies
- Realized or unrealized foreign-exchange gains
- Inflation, hyperinflation, redenomination, or currency collapse
- Physical gold held as property rather than gold used as a threshold calibration
- Migration or a prospective change in primary currency

Multiple native-currency subledgers may be useful bookkeeping components, but they should feed one consolidated personal history rather than silently become separate spiritual obligations. Otherwise individually sub-threshold currency balances could fail to reveal that the person's combined assessable possessions have reached a threshold.

## 2. Current-gold indexed credit

### What it tried to solve

The original gold-denominated experiment sought one consistent unit across currencies and dates. It translated previously assessed or inferred principal into gold units and compared those units with present assessable wealth expressed at the current gold price.

### Why it is not the default

A current translation of historical gold units is not the same operation as recording the gold-defined threshold at a historical assessment. As the [worked counterexamples](open-questions-and-counterexamples.md#2-historical-state-question-and-indexed-credit-counterexamples) show, using the translation as deductible principal can shelter a new acquisition when gold rises or manufacture an apparent increment when gold falls without an economic event. Inferring historical units from a later payment date is a separate legacy-reconstruction problem addressed below.

### Current use

The workbench may show the current-gold indexed result beside the provisional ordinary lens only when a user explicitly enables it as a counterfactual research comparison using the same immutable facts. It must label the result experimental, show the ordinary result and the gold-rise and gold-fall consequences, and never overwrite historical state, become the ordinary or recorded obligation, enter allocation, or appear as deductible assessed principal outside the counterfactual view.

## 3. Payment-derived gold units

### Historical form

An earlier ledger preserved payment amounts and gold prices associated with their payment dates, sometimes using a gold price retrieved retrospectively for the payment date. It derived a gold quantity of “purified” principal using a formula of the form:

`payment amount ÷ 19% ÷ payment-date gold value`

### Why it is a reconstruction rather than an assessment fact

A payment establishes that an amount was remitted. Without a separately preserved assessment, it does not establish the assessment date, assessable property, deductions, completed units, obligation currency, or whether assessment and payment occurred together. If payment was delayed, payment-date gold can produce a different unit count from the one that generated the obligation.

An imported legacy record should therefore preserve separately:

- Payment amount, currency, and date
- Gold-rate value and effective market date
- Whether the rate was captured contemporaneously or retrieved later, when known
- The legacy formula and result
- The unknown or estimated assessment facts
- Confidence and provenance of any reconstruction

This preserves the earlier understanding without manufacturing precision or preventing later reinterpretation.

## 4. Separate “purified wealth” spending account

### What it tried to solve

This approach segregated wealth on which Huqúq was understood to have been paid and used that account for non-needful or discretionary purchases. It attempted to make the once-only principle tangible, avoid accidentally spending uncleared funds on discretionary items, and trace cleared principal as it changed form.

### Reconstructed rationale for setting it aside as a default

The project's earlier counter-rationale was not preserved. The following is reconstructed from the current event, holding, and once-only model and should be reviewed rather than attributed to an earlier decision with certainty.

The once-only principle concerns a person's property and assessment history, not the label on a bank account. A transfer between the same owner's accounts creates no wealth, and exchanging assessed cash for another owned asset does not itself create an augmentation. The asset's current classification and the history of principal already assessed are related but different questions.

Treating the separate account as doctrinally necessary can therefore:

- Confuse cash segregation with the source-defined once-only principle
- Attach a permanent “purified” status to current asset rows when aggregate or event-level history may suffice
- Become arbitrary when money is commingled or a purchase has mixed funding
- Confuse consumption, which reduces owned property, with an exchange that leaves another asset in its place
- Turn a voluntary budgeting discipline into an implied prerequisite for discretionary spending

The approach may still be meaningful as a voluntary personal discipline or explanatory provenance tool. It is set aside only as the default calculation architecture and as a claimed requirement of the guidance.

## 5. How a posture changes

A posture may be changed when:

- A source or authoritative clarification directly addresses the question
- A counterexample reveals a substantive failure
- Consultation supplies a better-supported synthesis
- A simpler representation preserves the same constraints with less invented state
- New financial situations expose a limit that the earlier approach did not consider

When that happens, update this document with the new status, rationale, date, and affected examples. Do not erase the earlier approach or rewrite recorded financial facts. A methodology explorer should be able to replay the same fictional or private history under both interpretations and explain the exact reason their results differ.
